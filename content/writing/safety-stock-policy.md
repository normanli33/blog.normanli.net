## Problem

The textbook safety stock formula assumes you have already decided your
service level. In practice that decision — how often are we willing to stock
out, per SKU class, and who signs off on it — is the entire problem.

## Background

Demand variability and lead time variability are inputs. Service level is a
trade-off between working capital and customer promises, which makes it a
management policy, not a statistical parameter.

## Analysis

We classified SKUs by margin contribution and demand pattern, then priced the
incremental holding cost of each service-level tier so the trade-off was a
dollar figure rather than a z-score.

## Recommendation

Bring the service-level matrix to S&OP as a decision, with costs attached.
The formula comes after the signature.

## Lessons learned

Framing the same numbers as "policy options with prices" instead of
"statistical outputs" changed the meeting entirely.
